## Accounts Receivable Specialist

### What This Role Does
Accounts Receivable (AR) specialists manage the process of collecting payment from customers for goods or services already delivered. The role spans invoice generation, payment processing, cash application, collections (chasing overdue accounts), dispute resolution, and reconciliation. In SaaS businesses, AR sits at the intersection of finance, sales, and customer success — late payments often signal at-risk customer relationships, and the AR team is often the first to know.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Accounts Receivable Specialist, AR Specialist, Collections Specialist |
| Senior | Senior AR Specialist, AR Analyst |
| Manager | AR Manager, Accounts Receivable Manager, Credit Manager |
| Director | Head of AR, Director of Revenue Accounting, Controller (broader) |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Invoice management: generates, sends, and tracks customer invoices accurately and on schedule
- Cash application: matches incoming payments to open invoices — handles partial payments, overpayments, and misapplications
- Collections: proactive outreach on overdue accounts — calls and emails, escalating systematically
- Reconciliation: monthly reconciliation of AR ledger against bank statements and customer records
- AR aging analysis: monitors and reports on the AR aging schedule (30/60/90/120+ day buckets)

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- ERP/accounting software: NetSuite, Sage Intacct, QuickBooks, SAP, or Oracle — hands-on experience, not just data entry
- Credit management: evaluates customer creditworthiness, sets credit limits, reviews credit holds
- Dispute resolution: investigates billing disputes, coordinates with sales/CS, processes credit memos
- DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) management: tracks and works to reduce DSO as a key performance metric
- Process automation: has implemented or improved AR automation (billing tools, payment portals, dunning sequences)

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Revenue recognition knowledge: understands ASC 606 or IFRS 15 basics — important for SaaS billing
- Subscription billing platforms: Stripe Billing, Chargebee, Zuora, Recurly — especially relevant for SaaS
- High-volume AR: has managed 500+ active customer accounts simultaneously
- Has implemented a collections improvement programme with measurable DSO reduction

### Seniority Signals

- **Specialist → Senior:** Manages complex collections situations independently; produces AR aging reports and presents findings to the finance team
- **Senior → Manager:** Owns the full AR function; sets credit policy; manages disputes with commercial sensitivity (not just collections aggression)
- **Key signal:** "How do you approach collecting from a long-standing customer who is 60 days overdue?" Great candidates balance collection urgency with relationship preservation — they understand that aggressive collections can damage a customer relationship that sales/CS has built.

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Process walkthrough | Walk me through your collections process from invoice sent to payment received | Structure, escalation logic, communication approach |
| Dispute scenario | A customer is disputing a $10k invoice — they say they never received the service | Investigation process, cross-functional coordination, resolution path |
| DSO discussion | What was your DSO in your last role and what did you do to manage it? | Commercial awareness, metric ownership |
| System proficiency | What accounting or billing systems have you used? Walk me through your daily workflow | Depth vs. surface familiarity |
| Difficult customer | Tell me about the most challenging collections situation you've handled | Communication skills, persistence, problem-solving |

### Red Flags
- Collections approach is purely aggressive — no sensitivity to customer relationships
- Cannot explain what DSO is or how to calculate it
- Has never used an ERP or billing platform beyond basic data entry
- No reconciliation experience — "accounting does that"
- Dispute resolution is always escalated immediately — no independent resolution
