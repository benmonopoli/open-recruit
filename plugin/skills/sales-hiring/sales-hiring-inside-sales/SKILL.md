# Inside Sales — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Inside Sales

### What This Role Does
Inside sales representatives sell remotely (phone, video, email) to SMB and mid-market customers in a high-velocity, shorter-cycle motion. Unlike enterprise AEs who manage 6–18 month deals, inside sales reps may close 5–20 deals per month with ACV in the $1k–$20k range. Speed, efficiency, and volume are the defining characteristics — the best inside sales reps move quickly through pipeline and are comfortable with a transactional rhythm.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Representative | Inside Sales Representative, Sales Representative, Commercial Sales Rep |
| Senior | Senior Inside Sales Rep, SMB Account Executive, SMB AE |
| Team Lead | Inside Sales Team Lead |
| Manager | Inside Sales Manager, SMB Sales Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- High-volume activity: comfortable with 40–80+ outreach touchpoints per day — calls, emails, demos
- Rapid qualification: can qualify in or out within 15 minutes — no wasted time on bad-fit prospects
- Short-cycle closing: closes deals in 1–5 touchpoints; doesn't over-engineer a simple sale
- Demo delivery: runs efficient 20–30 minute demos focused on the customer's key use case
- CRM discipline: updates every interaction in real time — pipeline accuracy is non-negotiable

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Self-serve to sales conversion: has experience converting free or trial users to paid plans
- Objection handling: handles the top 5–10 common objections fluently without escalating
- Upsell instinct: identifies expansion opportunity at close or during onboarding call — captures additional ACV in the same motion
- Multi-product or multi-tier selling: sells across different pricing tiers confidently
- Trial management: can run a structured free trial and convert at the end

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has set and broken personal quota records — competitive self-motivation
- Consistently top 10–20% of team for quota attainment over multiple quarters
- Promoted from SDR — understands the full pipeline from prospecting to close
- Product-led motion experience: works with PQLs (product-qualified leads) rather than purely outbound

### Red Flags
- Demo runs 60+ minutes for a $5k deal — no velocity sense
- Has only done long-cycle sales and expects enterprise-style nurturing
- Avoids the phone — inside sales at SMB level often requires high call volume
- No evidence of consistent quota attainment over multiple quarters
- Cannot close without heavy discounting — discount dependency at SMB scale destroys margins
