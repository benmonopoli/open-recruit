- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Sales Enablement

### What This Role Does
Sales enablement professionals equip sales teams with the knowledge, content, tools, and processes to engage buyers effectively and close more deals. The role covers new hire onboarding, ongoing training, playbook development, sales content management, and technology adoption. The best enablement practitioners measure their impact on rep ramp time, quota attainment rates, and win rates — not just training completion.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Sales Enablement Specialist, Sales Trainer, Sales Training Coordinator |
| Manager | Sales Enablement Manager, Sales Readiness Manager |
| Senior | Senior Sales Enablement Manager, Head of Sales Enablement |
| Director | Director of Sales Enablement, VP Sales Enablement |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Sales process knowledge: has sold or spent significant time embedded with a sales team — cannot enable what you haven't experienced
- Programme design: can design a new hire onboarding programme from scratch — curriculum, delivery, assessment
- Content creation: produces sales playbooks, battle cards, objection-handling guides, and enablement decks that reps actually use
- Training facilitation: comfortable running workshops, role-plays, and group coaching sessions
- Sales methodology fluency: can teach MEDDPICC, Challenger, or Command of the Message — not just describe it

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Enablement platforms: Highspot, Seismic, Guru, or Lessonly/Workramp — content management and learning administration
- Call recording analysis: uses Gong or Chorus to identify skill gaps and coach at scale
- Ramp time measurement: tracks new hire time-to-first-deal and time-to-quota as primary success metrics
- Certification programmes: builds structured certifications for product knowledge, demo delivery, and methodology
- Manager enablement: trains frontline sales managers to coach — not just ICs

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has reduced rep ramp time with measurable data
- Competitive intelligence programme ownership
- Enablement at scale (100+ reps across multiple segments or regions)
- Revenue attribution for enablement programmes

### Red Flags
- Has never worked in sales — enablement without sales experience creates programmes reps ignore
- Measures success in content created or training sessions run — not rep performance outcomes
- Playbooks and battle cards are long, dense, and unused
- No call recording analysis — misses the biggest coaching signal available
- "The reps don't use the content" accepted as a rep problem rather than an enablement problem
