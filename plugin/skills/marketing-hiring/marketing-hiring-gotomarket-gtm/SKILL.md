# Go-to-Market (GTM) — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Go-to-Market (GTM)

### What This Role Does
GTM roles orchestrate the strategy and execution of bringing products to market — defining target segments, pricing, channel mix, launch sequencing, and the cross-functional coordination between product, marketing, and sales. Often senior-level or a dedicated function at larger companies. At smaller companies, GTM responsibilities typically sit within product marketing or the Head of Marketing. A standalone GTM role signals significant commercial complexity or product portfolio breadth.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | GTM Manager, Go-to-Market Manager |
| Senior | Senior GTM Manager, GTM Lead |
| Director | Head of GTM, Director of GTM Strategy, VP GTM |
| Exec | Chief Revenue Officer (CRO) — when GTM spans sales + marketing |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Market segmentation: can define ICP, segment by size/vertical/use case with data
- Launch orchestration: cross-functional programme management across product, marketing, sales, CS
- Pricing and packaging input: has contributed to or led pricing decisions
- Commercial modelling: understands unit economics — CAC, LTV, payback period, ACV
- Stakeholder management at C-level: comfort presenting GTM strategy to exec team and board

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Channel strategy: direct vs. partner vs. product-led — knows when and why each applies
- Sales enablement design: ensures sales team has what they need to execute the GTM strategy
- Market entry: has led expansion into a new segment, region, or buyer persona
- Competitive landscape analysis feeding into GTM positioning
- Revenue forecasting contribution

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- New category creation experience
- International market entry (GTM varies significantly by region)
- PLG + sales-assisted hybrid motion design
- M&A integration GTM experience

### Red Flags
- GTM experience is only launch planning — no market segmentation or commercial modelling
- Has not worked closely with sales — GTM that ignores the commercial motion is incomplete
- Treats GTM as synonymous with product marketing — the disciplines overlap but GTM is broader
- No experience with pricing decisions
