# Community Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Community Marketing

### What This Role Does
Community marketers build and activate communities of customers, advocates, and enthusiasts around a product or brand. The role spans online community management (Discord, Slack, Circle, Discourse), offline community events (meetups, user groups), advocacy programmes, and ambassador schemes. Strong community marketers understand that community is a long-term asset — the best ones have built audiences that become self-sustaining engines for retention, referral, and product feedback.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Community Coordinator, Community Moderator |
| Manager | Community Manager, Community Marketing Manager |
| Senior | Senior Community Manager, Head of Community |
| Director | Director of Community, VP Community |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Community platform management: Discord, Slack, Circle, Discourse, or forum/Reddit-adjacent communities
- Member engagement: proactive conversation starting, content programming, event coordination
- Moderation: community guidelines enforcement, conflict resolution, toxic behaviour management
- Community analytics: member growth, engagement rate, content performance, churn
- Empathy and authenticity: the best community managers genuinely care about the community they serve

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Community programme design: ambassador programmes, superuser programmes, advocacy tiers
- Content strategy for community: AMAs, weekly threads, community challenges, product feedback loops
- Onboarding design: turning new members into engaged participants
- Community-to-product feedback: structured mechanisms for surfacing insights to product teams
- Events: community meetups, virtual events, user conferences

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Community-led growth: demonstrated community as a driver of product acquisition
- Building a community from zero (greenfield experience)
- Monetisation or sponsorship of community programmes
- Developer or technical community experience

### Red Flags
- Community management is moderation-only — reactive, not proactive
- No programme design experience — just answering questions in a Slack channel
- Cannot demonstrate engagement growth or retention impact
- Has never built an ambassador or advocacy programme
- Treats community as a support channel, not a growth asset
