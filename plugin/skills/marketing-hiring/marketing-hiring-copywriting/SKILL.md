# Copywriting — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Copywriting

### What This Role Does
Copywriters craft words that make people think, feel, and act. Unlike content marketing (which informs and educates), copywriting is primarily persuasive — ads, landing pages, email subject lines, onboarding flows, product pages, and CTAs. The best copywriters understand psychology, test obsessively, and can adapt their voice across channels and audiences. UX writing (microcopy, UI text) is adjacent but distinct.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Junior | Junior Copywriter, Copywriter, Marketing Writer |
| Mid | Copywriter, Senior Copywriter, Digital Copywriter |
| Senior | Senior Copywriter, Lead Copywriter |
| Director | Copy Director, Head of Copy, Creative Director (copy-led) |
| Adjacent | UX Writer, Content Designer, Conversion Copywriter |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Portfolio of persuasive writing: ads, landing pages, email, CTAs — not just articles
- Understanding of audience psychology: benefits vs. features, objection handling, social proof
- Headline and hook writing: can write 10 versions and identify the strongest
- Brand voice adaptability: can write in multiple registers across clients or products
- Editing: their own and others' copy — concise, clear, direct

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- A/B testing: has run copy tests and can articulate what works and why
- Conversion copywriting: landing pages, product pages, checkout flows — with CRO awareness
- Email copywriting: subject lines, preview text, body copy, CTAs — deliverability awareness
- Ad copywriting: Google Ads character limits, Meta ad formats, LinkedIn sponsored copy
- UX microcopy: button labels, error messages, empty states, tooltips

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Direct response copywriting background (high-conversion direct mail / email)
- Demand gen copy: can write for full funnel, not just top of funnel
- Copy strategy: can develop a messaging hierarchy, not just execute briefs
- Published work with measurable lift data

### Seniority Signals

- **Junior → Mid:** Requires less brief detail; first draft is close to final; can self-edit
- **Mid → Senior:** Pushes back on briefs that won't convert; brings strategic thinking to copy approach; mentors junior writers
- **Senior → Director:** Sets copy standards and voice guidelines; leads creative reviews; connects copy strategy to brand and business goals

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio review | Review their portfolio before the call — assess range and quality | Depth, not just volume |
| Live copy task | Write 3 subject line variations and a 100-word landing page section for [product] | Voice, speed, confidence |
| Copy critique | Here's a real page of ours — rewrite the hero section | Diagnosis and improvement instinct |
| Testing discussion | Walk me through a copy test you've run — what did you learn? | Experimentation mindset |

### Red Flags
- Portfolio is all long-form content — no short, punchy persuasive copy
- Cannot explain why they made specific word choices
- No evidence of testing or iteration
- Defensive about edits — copy is always collaborative
- Generic, bland voice — sounds like every other brand
