# Social Media Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Social Media Marketing

### What This Role Does
Social media marketers build and grow brand presence across platforms, create platform-native content, manage communities, and drive audience engagement. The role has evolved significantly — it now requires platform algorithm fluency, short-form video production, real-time content instincts, and analytics rigour. Strong social marketers are part brand voice, part content creator, part community manager.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Social Media Coordinator, Social Media Assistant, Community Coordinator |
| Manager | Social Media Manager, Social Media Marketer |
| Senior | Senior Social Media Manager, Social Media Strategist |
| Director | Director of Social Media, Head of Social, Social Media Director |
| Specialist | Creator/Influencer Coordinator, Community Manager (platform-specific) |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Platform fluency: LinkedIn, Instagram, X/Twitter, TikTok, YouTube — knows each platform's distinct audience behaviour and algorithm
- Content creation: writing posts, basic image/graphic creation, short-form video (at least scripting and directing if not production)
- Brand voice: can maintain consistent tone across platforms and content types
- Scheduling and publishing tools: Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social, Later
- Social analytics: platform-native analytics plus social listening tools

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Short-form video production: filming, editing (CapCut, Premiere, DaVinci), on-camera comfort or directing others
- Social listening: Brandwatch, Mention, Sprout — monitoring brand and competitor mentions
- Community management: genuine, human responses that build audience loyalty
- Paid social (basic): boosting posts, understanding audience targeting
- Trend identification: spotting what's working in their niche before it saturates

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Viral content track record (substantiated, not claimed)
- Creator/influencer relationship network
- Social-to-revenue attribution (connecting social activity to pipeline or sales)
- LinkedIn B2B thought leadership strategy
- TikTok/YouTube Shorts native creator experience

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Runs the content calendar independently; develops creative concepts, not just executes briefs
- **Manager → Senior:** Can demonstrate meaningful audience or engagement growth with data; makes strategic platform bets
- **Senior → Director:** Manages a social team or agency; connects social to brand and business outcomes; has navigated a brand crisis or controversy

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio review | Show me 5 posts you're most proud of and explain why | Range, quality, platform fluency, self-awareness |
| Channel audit | Do a live audit of our social channels — what would you change? | Strategic instincts, diagnosis ability |
| Content brief | Write 3 LinkedIn post concepts for [topic] in our brand voice | Voice adaptability, creative range |
| Analytics walkthrough | Walk me through a campaign that performed well and one that didn't | Data literacy, learning mindset |

### Red Flags
- Portfolio is all static graphics — no video, no personality
- Engagement metrics are vanity-focused (impressions only, no engagement rate or click analysis)
- Uses the same content across all platforms without adaptation
- No understanding of any one platform's algorithm at a meaningful depth
- "I manage the posting schedule" — coordination, not strategy
