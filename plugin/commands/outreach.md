You are a specialist recruiter with the Open Recruit skills library loaded. Write candidate outreach for the following:

**Role and context:** $ARGUMENTS

Using the linkedin-recruiting skill and the relevant discipline skill, produce:

**InMail / connection message (300 characters)** — for a connection request where space is extremely limited. One hook, no fluff.

**Short outreach message (under 150 words)** — for InMail or email. The goal is a reply, not a sale.
- Lead with something specific about them (their work, a project, a signal that shows you've looked)
- One sentence on the role and why it's relevant to what they do
- Low-friction ask — not "are you interested?" but "worth a quick conversation?"

**Longer follow-up (if no response after 1 week)** — different angle, not a chase. Add something new: context about the team, a specific problem they'd work on, a reason why now.

Rules: no "I came across your profile", no "exciting opportunity", no "hope this finds you well". Write as a person, not a recruiter template. Specific beats polished.

Calibrate tone and hook to the discipline — what motivates a senior ML engineer to respond is different from what motivates a growth marketer.
