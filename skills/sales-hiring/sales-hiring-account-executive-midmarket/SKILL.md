# Account Executive — Mid-Market — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDPICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Account Executive — Mid-Market

### What This Role Does
Mid-market AEs manage the full sales cycle for deals in the $10k–$100k ACV range, typically with 30–90 day sales cycles. They prospect (sometimes), run discovery, demo, propose, negotiate, and close. At SaaS companies with PLG motions, mid-market AEs often work expansion deals — upgrading product-qualified accounts (PQAs) from self-serve to paid plans.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| AE | Account Executive, Commercial Account Executive, Mid-Market AE |
| Senior | Senior Account Executive, Senior Mid-Market AE |
| Lead | Lead Account Executive |
| PLG-specific | Expansion AE, Product Sales Specialist, Growth AE |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Full sales cycle ownership: discovery → demo → proposal → negotiation → close
- Discovery quality: asks second and third-level questions to uncover real business pain, not surface needs
- Demo delivery: tailored, outcomes-focused demos — not feature tours
- Pipeline management: accurate CRM hygiene, realistic forecasting, urgency creation
- Quota attainment: consistent track record — ask for quarterly attainment numbers

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- MEDDIC or BANT depth: can qualify out quickly without burning relationships
- Multi-threading: builds relationships with champions and economic buyers, not just the first contact
- Competitive positioning: knows how to handle objections from named competitors
- Trial/POC management: runs structured trials with clear success criteria and exit criteria
- Negotiation: comfortable discussing pricing, terms, and discount governance without escalating immediately

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Outbound prospecting skills (for full-cycle AE roles)
- PLG expansion motion: has converted self-serve users to enterprise/team plans
- Partner-sourced deal experience
- Has closed deals > $50k ACV in a mid-market motion

### Seniority Signals

- **AE → Senior AE:** Consistently at or above quota for 4+ consecutive quarters; mentors newer AEs; handles larger or more complex deals within the segment
- **Senior AE → Manager:** Has coached peers without being asked; has strong opinions on what works in the sales process; wants to develop a team, not just hit their own number
- **Green flag:** President's Club attainment — most companies award this to top 10-20% of performers

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Quota deep dive | Walk me through your attainment at each role — exact numbers | Accountability, trend, deal size progression |
| Discovery roleplay | Run discovery on me as a [target persona] | Question quality, listening, pain identification |
| Demo assessment | Demo our product to me (give them 30 min prep) | How they tailor, whether they lead with features or outcomes |
| Deal review | Walk me through your last big win and your last big loss | Process quality, self-awareness, attribution |
| Forecast discussion | How do you manage your pipeline and forecast to your manager? | CRM discipline, forecasting accuracy |

### Red Flags
- Cannot give specific quota attainment numbers — "roughly" or "around" is evasion
- Discovery is a checklist, not a conversation
- Demo is a product tour — no discovery used, no tailoring
- Has never closed a deal without heavy manager involvement
- Inconsistent attainment with no explanation — one great quarter surrounded by misses
- Blames quota, territory, or product for misses exclusively
