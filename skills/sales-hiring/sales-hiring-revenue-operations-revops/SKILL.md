# Revenue Operations (RevOps) — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Revenue Operations (RevOps)

### What This Role Does
Revenue Operations professionals align sales, marketing, and customer success under a unified operational framework — shared data, shared attribution, shared tech stack, and shared revenue metrics. Where Sales Ops focuses on the sales team, RevOps spans the full revenue funnel from first marketing touch to customer renewal. It's the fastest-growing operations function in B2B SaaS. The goal is to eliminate the handoff failures and data silos that slow revenue growth.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | RevOps Specialist, Revenue Operations Analyst, GTM Operations Specialist |
| Manager | RevOps Manager, Revenue Operations Manager |
| Senior | Senior RevOps Manager, Head of Revenue Operations |
| Director | Director of Revenue Operations, VP Revenue Operations |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Salesforce (SFDC): core administration — objects, workflows, reports, dashboards, data integrity
- HubSpot or Marketo: marketing automation CRM integration — bi-directional sync, lead lifecycle management
- Full-funnel visibility: can trace a lead from first marketing touch through closed-won opportunity to renewal
- Attribution modelling: understands first-touch, last-touch, and multi-touch attribution — and their limitations
- Cross-functional communication: bridges sales, marketing, and CS — translates each team's language to the others

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Revenue forecasting: builds models that combine marketing pipeline contribution, sales velocity, and CS expansion
- Tech stack rationalisation: has audited and simplified an over-tooled GTM stack
- CDP/data infrastructure: Segment, Rudderstack, or equivalent — understanding of customer data architecture
- SQL: custom queries for cross-system analysis (Salesforce + HubSpot + product analytics)
- Lead routing and scoring: complex routing rules, intent-based scoring, ABM account matching

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has designed and implemented a RevOps function from scratch
- Revenue intelligence: Clari, Gong, or similar for cross-functional forecasting
- BI tool administration: Looker, Tableau, or Metabase for executive revenue dashboards
- PLG metrics: product-qualified lead (PQL) scoring, self-serve to sales conversion tracking

### Seniority Signals

- **Specialist → Manager:** Can independently map the full revenue funnel, identify the biggest leakage points, and propose solutions
- **Manager → Director:** Owns the revenue data model; influences tech stack investment decisions; presents revenue operations metrics to the board alongside the CRO

### Red Flags
- Treats RevOps as "Sales Ops with a new name" — no marketing or CS operational experience
- Cannot explain the difference between a MQL and a PQL or how lead scoring works
- Tech stack experience is only one system (pure Salesforce admins often struggle with the breadth RevOps requires)
- No attribution experience — "marketing uses HubSpot, sales uses Salesforce" with no integration
- Has never run a revenue forecast across functions
