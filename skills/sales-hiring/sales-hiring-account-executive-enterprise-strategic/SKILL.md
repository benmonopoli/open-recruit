- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDPICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Account Executive — Enterprise / Strategic

### What This Role Does
Enterprise AEs manage large, complex deals with $100k+ ACV, 3–18 month sales cycles, and multiple stakeholders across the buying committee. The role requires executive relationship skills, political navigation, procurement and legal fluency, and deep patience. Enterprise AEs often carry fewer accounts but must go deeper — understanding the customer's business, industry, and strategic priorities well enough to connect the product to board-level outcomes.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| AE | Enterprise Account Executive, Strategic Account Executive, Named Account Executive |
| Senior | Senior Enterprise AE, Senior Strategic Account Executive |
| Director | Director of Enterprise Sales (often player-coach) |
| Specialist | Global Account Executive, Major Accounts Executive |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- MEDDPICC depth: can map the complete picture — economic buyer, champion, decision criteria, paper process, competition — for every active deal
- Executive relationship management: comfortable calling and meeting C-suite and VP-level contacts, not just champions
- Multi-stakeholder navigation: can manage a buying committee of 6–15 people with different agendas
- Complex deal structuring: multi-year agreements, phased rollouts, volume commitments
- Long-cycle pipeline management: can sustain momentum on deals lasting 6-18 months without losing urgency

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Procurement and legal fluency: has navigated enterprise security reviews, legal redlines, and procurement processes without blocking deals
- Champion development: actively coaches internal champions on how to sell internally on the vendor's behalf
- Competitive displacement: has won against entrenched incumbents — understands how to reframe evaluation criteria
- Business case construction: can build or co-build an ROI model with the customer's own numbers
- Executive presentation: comfortable delivering in boardroom settings; adapts message to audience level

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- 7-figure deal experience ($1M+ ACV or TCV)
- Greenfield territory built from zero (no inherited book of business)
- Global/multinational deal experience — multiple countries, currencies, entities
- Industry vertical expertise (BFSI, healthcare, government) adding domain credibility
- Has used Gong/Chorus to review and coach their own deals

### Seniority Signals

- **AE → Senior Enterprise AE:** Has closed multiple $250k+ deals; runs a clean MEDDPICC on every deal without being asked; coaches peers on deal strategy
- **Senior → Director:** Has built a team or mentored 3+ AEs to quota attainment; influences sales process and methodology at the org level
- **Critical question:** "Walk me through the biggest deal you've ever closed — from first meeting to signature." Great enterprise AEs give a detailed answer covering the full buying committee, the champion, the obstacles, and exactly what they did at each stage.

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| MEDDPICC drill | Take me through your current #1 deal using MEDDPICC | Qualification depth, where deals actually are vs. where they're perceived to be |
| Executive presence | Present your vision for our enterprise segment to me as the CEO | Poise, commercial storytelling, boardroom readiness |
| Deal loss analysis | Walk me through a big deal you lost — why, and what you'd do differently | Self-awareness, learning mindset, attribution accuracy |
| Champion coaching | How do you prepare your champion to sell internally without you in the room? | Strategic thinking, multi-threading |
| Territory plan | Give them a target account list: build a 90-day territory plan | Prioritisation, account strategy, research instincts |

### Red Flags
- MEDDPICC knowledge is theoretical — cannot apply it to a real deal they're working
- Has only sold deals under $50k — deal complexity doesn't transfer linearly
- Relies on product to sell itself — no consultative or challenger approach
- All deals sourced by SDRs — no evidence of self-sourcing capability
- Seniority mismatch: long tenure as enterprise AE but cannot name a deal they strategically engineered
