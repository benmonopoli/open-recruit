- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Renewal Manager

### What This Role Does
Renewal Managers own the operational and commercial process of renewing existing customer contracts. At SaaS companies with large customer bases, this is a dedicated role — distinct from Account Management (which focuses on expansion) and Customer Success (which focuses on adoption). Renewal Managers handle renewal forecasting, commercial negotiation, multi-year deal structuring, and escalations, with the goal of maximising net revenue retention (NRR) at renewal.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Renewal Specialist, Renewal Representative, Renewals Coordinator |
| Manager | Renewal Manager, Commercial Renewal Manager |
| Senior | Senior Renewal Manager, Head of Renewals |
| Director | Director of Renewals, VP Renewals |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Renewal forecasting: maintains an accurate 90-day rolling renewal forecast — no surprises
- At-risk identification: can spot churn signals early (low usage, open support tickets, champion departure) and engages a save process proactively
- Commercial negotiation: handles price increases, term changes, and competitive pressures confidently at renewal
- Multi-year structuring: can construct and propose multi-year deals that reduce future churn risk
- CRM hygiene: renewal dates, opportunity amounts, and stage accuracy in Salesforce or equivalent

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- NRR track record: can demonstrate net revenue retention figures from previous roles
- Collaboration with CSM: has a structured handoff and co-ownership model with CS for at-risk accounts
- Escalation management: knows when and how to escalate to leadership without using it as a crutch
- Legal and procurement fluency: has navigated contract redlines and procurement delays without missing renewal dates
- Expansion at renewal: converts flat renewals into upsells during the renewal process

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has managed a renewal book above $5M ARR
- Renewal operations design: built renewal playbooks, forecast cadences, and escalation processes
- Churn win-back: has successfully re-signed churned customers

### Red Flags
- Renewal process starts at 30 days — at-risk accounts need 90+ day lead time
- No forecast accuracy data or history — "I close what I can"
- Cannot explain their approach to a price-increase negotiation
- Relies entirely on CSM to identify at-risk accounts — no independent monitoring
- Has not closed a meaningful multi-year deal at renewal
