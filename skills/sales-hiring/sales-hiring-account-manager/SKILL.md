# Account Manager — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Account Manager

### What This Role Does
Account managers own the commercial relationship with existing customers — managing renewals, identifying and closing expansion (upsell/cross-sell), and ensuring the customer is getting value. The role sits between sales (commercial focus) and customer success (adoption/retention focus). At some companies these overlap significantly — clarify whether your AM role is primarily commercial (quota-carrying expansion) or relationship-focused (success-adjacent) before sourcing.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | Account Manager, Client Manager, Commercial Account Manager |
| Senior | Senior Account Manager, Strategic Account Manager |
| Director | Director of Account Management, Head of Account Management |
| Adjacent | Client Partner, Client Success Manager (commercial-focused), Growth Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Relationship management: builds genuine trust with customer stakeholders, not just transactional contact
- Account planning: maps accounts for whitespace, at-risk signals, and expansion opportunities
- Renewal execution: proactively manages renewals — no surprises at renewal date
- Upsell/cross-sell: identifies and closes expansion opportunities within the existing customer base
- Commercial negotiation: handles multi-year renewals, price increases, and expansion terms confidently

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- QBRs: runs effective Quarterly Business Reviews — data-driven, customer-focused, forward-looking
- Multi-threading: builds relationships beyond the primary contact — into finance, IT, legal, and the C-suite
- At-risk account management: identifies churn risk early and has a structured save process
- CRM and forecasting: maintains accurate account data and expansion forecast
- Customer advocacy: converts satisfied customers into references, case studies, and testimonials

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) track record above 120%
- Has managed a book of business above $5M ARR
- Executive-level relationships in named account portfolios
- Has rebuilt a churned or at-risk account relationship

### Seniority Signals

- **AM → Senior AM:** Manages a larger or more strategic book; runs QBRs independently; forecasts expansion pipeline accurately
- **Senior AM → Director:** Manages a team of AMs or a portfolio of top strategic accounts; designs account management processes and playbooks

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Account planning exercise | Given this (anonymised) account — build a 90-day account plan | Strategic thinking, whitespace identification |
| Renewal scenario | A customer is pushing back on a 15% price increase at renewal — walk me through how you handle it | Negotiation, value reframing, escalation judgement |
| Expansion deal walk-through | Tell me about the best expansion deal you've sourced and closed | Commercial instincts, deal ownership |
| At-risk scenario | A customer's champion just left. What do you do? | Risk management, multi-threading |
| QBR review | Walk me through a QBR you've run — what did you cover, and what was the outcome? | Customer-facing quality, commercial focus |

### Red Flags
- Renewal management is reactive — only engages at the 30-day mark
- "Relationships are good" with no data on health or expansion pipeline
- Has never closed a meaningful upsell — AM role is commercial, not just relationship maintenance
- No account planning process — operates reactively to inbound requests
- Poor churn ownership: "the product wasn't right" without any save attempt
