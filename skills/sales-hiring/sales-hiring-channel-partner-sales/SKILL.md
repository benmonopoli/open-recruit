# Channel / Partner Sales — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Channel / Partner Sales

### What This Role Does
Channel and partner sales professionals drive revenue through indirect channels — resellers, system integrators, agencies, technology partners, and distribution networks. Unlike direct sales, the role requires enabling and motivating someone else's team to sell on your behalf. Success depends on partner selection, enablement quality, co-selling execution, and programme design. For SaaS companies with agency ecosystems (common in SEO, marketing tech, and developer tools), channel sales is a significant growth lever.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | Channel Sales Manager, Partner Sales Manager, Agency Partnerships Manager |
| Senior | Senior Channel Manager, Strategic Partner Manager |
| Director | Head of Channel, Director of Partner Sales, VP Channel |
| Adjacent | Alliances Manager, ISV Partner Manager, Reseller Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Partner recruitment: can identify, qualify, and sign new channel partners — outbound prospecting for partners, not just managing inbound
- Partner enablement: builds and delivers training so partners can sell and support the product independently
- Co-selling motion: comfortable joining partner calls as the vendor overlay — supporting without taking over
- Partner pipeline management: tracks partner-sourced and partner-influenced pipeline separately from direct
- Commercial structure: understands reseller margins, referral fees, and MDF (market development funds) allocation

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Partner programme design: has designed or evolved a tiered partner programme (Silver/Gold/Platinum) with clear criteria and benefits
- Agency partner experience: specific experience with marketing, digital, or SEO agencies as resellers/referrers — relevant for SEO tooling
- Technology (ISV) partnerships: integration partner programmes, marketplace listings
- Partner portal management: has administered a partner portal and kept it actively used
- Channel conflict management: has navigated and resolved direct vs. channel territory conflicts

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has built a channel programme from zero
- International channel: partner networks across EMEA, APAC, or LATAM
- GSI (Global System Integrator) experience: Deloitte, Accenture, Wipro-level relationships
- Demonstrated partner-sourced ARR at significant scale ($2M+)

### Red Flags
- No outbound partner recruitment — only manages inbound, pre-existing partners
- Cannot demonstrate partner-attributed revenue with data
- Partner enablement is a one-time onboarding call — no ongoing programme
- No experience managing channel conflict with direct sales team
- "Partners aren't engaged" accepted without diagnosing why
