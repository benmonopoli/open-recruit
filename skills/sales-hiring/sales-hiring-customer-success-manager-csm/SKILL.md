# Customer Success Manager (CSM) — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |

| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Customer Success Manager (CSM)

### What This Role Does
Customer Success Managers ensure customers achieve the outcomes they bought the product for — driving adoption, reducing churn, and identifying expansion opportunities. The role is proactive (not reactive support) and commercially aware (NRR is a key CSM metric alongside retention). At PLG companies, CSMs often work with the highest-value self-serve accounts or serve as the bridge from product-qualified to sales-qualified. The best CSMs are part consultant, part coach, part commercial partner.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Associate | Associate CSM, Customer Success Associate |
| Manager | Customer Success Manager, Client Success Manager |
| Senior | Senior CSM, Strategic CSM, Enterprise CSM |
| Director | Head of Customer Success, Director of Customer Success, VP Customer Success |
| Adjacent | Customer Onboarding Manager, Implementation Manager, Technical CSM |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Onboarding: can take a new customer from signed contract to first value realised — structured, time-bound, with clear milestones
- Health scoring: monitors product usage, engagement, and sentiment to identify at-risk accounts before they churn
- Renewal management: owns or contributes to renewal — knows the renewal date, the value story, and the stakeholder map
- Product knowledge depth: cannot add value to customers without genuinely understanding the product
- Customer communication: clear, professional, proactive outreach — not waiting for customers to come to them

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- QBR design and delivery: data-driven business reviews focused on customer outcomes and mutual roadmap
- Expansion identification: can spot upsell/cross-sell opportunities and either close them or hand to AM/AE with a warm intro
- CS platforms: Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango, or Salesforce CS — health scoring, playbook execution, renewal tracking
- Customer segmentation: understands how to manage high-touch (1:1), tech-touch (1:many), and digital-touch CSM motions
- NPS/CSAT programmes: collects and acts on customer feedback systematically

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Technical CSM: comfortable with APIs, integrations, and light technical troubleshooting for technical products
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) above 110% track record
- Has built or significantly improved a CS programme
- Community or advocacy programme ownership
- Executive relationship management at enterprise accounts

### Seniority Signals

- **Associate → CSM:** Manages a portfolio independently; handles at-risk accounts without escalating everything; runs QBRs confidently
- **CSM → Senior / Enterprise CSM:** Manages larger, more complex accounts; influences product roadmap through customer feedback; expansion revenue attribution
- **Senior → Director:** Builds the CS function and team; designs health scoring and playbooks; owns NRR at the programme level; reports commercial CS outcomes to leadership

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| At-risk scenario | A customer hasn't logged in for 45 days, renewal is in 60 days — what do you do? | Proactive instinct, save process |
| QBR roleplay | Run a QBR with me as a customer who's somewhat disengaged | Confidence, data use, forward-looking framing |
| Expansion scenario | You notice a customer is using 80% of their user seats — how do you handle it? | Commercial awareness, escalation vs. ownership |
| Onboarding design | Walk me through how you'd onboard a new [our ICP] customer | Structure, milestone-setting, value realisation focus |
| Metrics discussion | What metrics do you use to measure your own success as a CSM? | Outcome-orientation, NRR awareness |

### Red Flags
- CS is "making customers happy" with no commercial or retention accountability
- No proactive customer outreach — only responds to support tickets
- Cannot explain what health scoring is or how they've used it
- Has never owned or influenced a renewal
- No product knowledge depth — describes the product at a surface level
- Treats churn as "the product's fault" without examining their own role
