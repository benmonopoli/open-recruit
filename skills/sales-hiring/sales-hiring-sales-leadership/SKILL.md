# Sales Leadership — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Sales Leadership

### What This Role Does
Sales leaders own revenue outcomes for a team, segment, or the entire company. They hire and develop reps, manage pipeline and forecasting, set quota and territory strategy, and work cross-functionally with marketing, product, and CS to align the revenue engine. The best sales leaders are data-driven coaches — they use call recordings, pipeline data, and win/loss analysis to make their team better, not just to report numbers upward.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | Sales Manager, SDR Manager, Regional Sales Manager |
| Director | Sales Director, Director of Sales, Regional Director |
| VP | VP Sales, VP of Revenue, VP of Commercial |
| Head | Head of Sales |
| C-Suite | Chief Revenue Officer (CRO), Chief Sales Officer (CSO) |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Pipeline management: can accurately forecast to within ±10% of quarter-end close — has a structured deal review process
- Coaching: genuinely develops reps — uses call recordings, role-plays, and structured 1:1s; not just reporting on performance
- Hiring: has built a team, including identifying talent, interviewing, and ramping new hires to quota
- Quota and territory design: understands how to set motivating but achievable quotas; has input on territory carving
- Commercial literacy: understands CAC, LTV, sales velocity, win rates, and ASP — speaks the language of the board

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Sales methodology implementation: has adopted and embedded a formal methodology (MEDDPICC, Challenger, Command of the Message) across a team
- Cross-functional alignment: partners effectively with marketing (pipeline SLAs), product (feedback loops), and CS (handoff quality)
- Comp plan design: has contributed to or designed sales compensation plans that drive the right behaviours
- Performance management: has managed underperformers through a formal PIP process — not avoided it
- Hiring at scale: has built teams of 10+ and maintained high performance standards through growth

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has built a sales function from near-scratch (first sales hire or first sales leader)
- International sales leadership: multi-region, multi-currency, multi-segment
- Board-level presentation: has presented commercial performance and outlook at board level
- Revenue diversification: has added a new segment, channel, or geography under their leadership
- CRO-level: has owned full revenue including CS, partnerships, and self-serve in addition to direct sales

### Seniority Signals

- **Manager → Director:** Manages a team of AEs not just a territory; contributes to comp plan and quota discussions; has hired and fired independently
- **Director → VP:** Owns a segment P&L (e.g. mid-market or enterprise); has built a team of managers; reports to CRO or CEO; has exceeded segment quota for multiple consecutive years
- **VP → CRO:** Owns all revenue streams including CS, partnerships, and self-serve; sits on the leadership team; has board-level credibility; has led a company through a scale or inflection point

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline review | Walk me through your current team's pipeline — how do you forecast close? | Process rigour, forecasting accuracy, deal visibility |
| Coaching scenario | One of your AEs is at 60% of quota in month 2 of Q3. Walk me through what you do | Coaching specificity — not "I'd have a conversation" |
| Hiring philosophy | What do you look for in an AE hire and how do you assess it? | Talent eye, hiring process, quality bar |
| Revenue narrative | Walk me through a year where you exceeded team quota — what drove it? | Attribution, repeatability, strategic thinking |
| 30/60/90 presentation | Present your plan for the first 90 days in this role | Prioritisation, stakeholder management, commercial instincts |

### Red Flags
- Cannot explain their forecast process beyond "gut feel" or "whatever Salesforce shows"
- Coaching is inspection, not development — reviews numbers but doesn't improve behaviours
- Has never fired a persistent underperformer — avoids performance management
- All success attributed to the team, all failure attributed to the market — no ownership
- Title inflation: "VP Sales" at a 5-person startup managing one AE is not equivalent to VP Sales at a 200-person org
