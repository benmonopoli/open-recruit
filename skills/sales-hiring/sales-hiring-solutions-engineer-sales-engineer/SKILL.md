# Solutions Engineer / Sales Engineer — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Solutions Engineer / Sales Engineer

### What This Role Does
Solutions Engineers (SEs) — also called Pre-Sales Engineers or Sales Engineers — are the technical bridge between the sales team and the customer. They run deep product demos, manage proof-of-concepts (POCs), respond to technical RFPs, and address security and integration questions that AEs cannot answer alone. For technical products, SEs are often the difference between winning and losing an enterprise deal. They are engineers who can sell, or salespeople who can code — both profiles exist and suit different products.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Associate | Associate Solutions Engineer, Junior Pre-Sales Engineer |
| Mid | Solutions Engineer (SE), Sales Engineer, Pre-Sales Engineer |
| Senior | Senior Solutions Engineer, Senior Sales Engineer |
| Director | Head of Solutions Engineering, Director of Pre-Sales |
| Adjacent | Solutions Architect (pre-sales), Technical Account Executive, Demo Engineer |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Product depth: knows the product at a technical level — architecture, APIs, integrations, limitations, and edge cases
- Demo delivery: runs tailored, outcome-focused demos that connect technical features to business value
- Technical credibility: can hold a conversation with a customer's engineering team without losing credibility
- POC management: scopes, executes, and drives POCs to a clear decision with defined success criteria
- Discovery: understands enough about the customer's technical environment to qualify the fit before demoing

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- RFP/RFI responses: has written detailed technical sections of enterprise RFP responses under time pressure
- Integration experience: can speak to how the product integrates with common customer tech stacks (CRMs, ERPs, data tools)
- Security and compliance: can address infosec questionnaires, SOC 2, ISO 27001, and data residency questions
- Custom demo environments: can build tailored sandbox environments with customer data (anonymised) for compelling demos
- Competitive technical positioning: knows the technical weaknesses of competitors and how to reframe the evaluation

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Coding ability to build custom integrations or demo scripts
- Has co-authored product documentation or helped shape the product roadmap based on pre-sales findings
- Industry vertical expertise (e.g. BFSI, healthcare) adding domain credibility
- Experience with API-first products and developer audiences

### Seniority Signals

- **Associate → SE:** Runs demos independently; manages a POC without AE hand-holding; handles infosec questionnaires alone
- **SE → Senior SE:** Handles the most complex technical deals; is the go-to for competitive situations; builds internal enablement content for the SE team
- **Senior → Director:** Builds and manages an SE team; defines the technical sales process; measures POC win rates and conversion

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Product demo | Demo our product to me as a [target persona] — 30 min prep | Discovery use, tailoring, technical depth, business value connection |
| Technical drill | Deep technical Q&A on our product or their current product | Real depth vs. surface-level knowledge |
| POC design | Design a POC for a [realistic customer scenario] — scope, criteria, timeline | Structure, success criteria definition, risk identification |
| RFP exercise | Here's a technical RFP question — draft a response | Writing quality, technical accuracy, completeness |
| Objection handling | "Your security doesn't meet our requirements" — how do you respond? | Technical confidence, process for addressing blockers |

### Red Flags
- Demo is a product tour — feature-by-feature, not tailored to the customer's pain
- Cannot explain the product's architecture or API without reading documentation in front of you
- Has never managed a POC without the AE driving it
- No ability to write — RFP responses require clear technical writing
- Dismissive of non-technical stakeholders — SEs must bridge both worlds
