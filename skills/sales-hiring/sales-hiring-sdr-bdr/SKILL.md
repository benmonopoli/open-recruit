# Sales Development Representative (SDR) / Business Development Representative (BDR) — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Sales Development Representative (SDR) / Business Development Representative (BDR)

### What This Role Does
SDRs and BDRs generate qualified pipeline through outbound prospecting and/or inbound lead qualification. They book meetings for Account Executives — they do not close deals. The SDR role is the entry point to most B2B sales careers and a proving ground for future AEs. BDR is often used interchangeably, though some companies use BDR specifically for outbound prospecting and SDR for inbound qualification.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Entry | SDR, BDR, Sales Development Representative, Business Development Representative |
| Senior | Senior SDR, Senior BDR, Lead SDR |
| Team lead | SDR Team Lead, SDR Manager (player-coach) |
| Adjacent | Market Development Representative (MDR), Outbound Development Representative |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Prospecting discipline: consistent daily activity — calls, emails, LinkedIn outreach — without needing to be chased
- Cold outreach: can write a compelling, personalised cold email and deliver a cold call opener without a script
- Qualification: understands BANT or SPICED — can identify fit and urgency, not just hand over any warm body
- CRM hygiene: logs every activity, updates prospect records accurately (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Resilience: handles rejection without internalising it — SDR is a high-rejection, high-volume role

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Sequencing tools: Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo, or similar — sequence design, A/B testing subject lines, step optimisation
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator: advanced search, saved leads, InMail, social selling
- Personalisation at scale: researches accounts/contacts and adapts messaging beyond template + name swap
- Multi-threading: engages multiple stakeholders at a target account, not just the first person who responds
- Call recording review: uses Gong or Chorus to self-coach on call performance

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Pipeline contribution beyond quota — consistently converts SDR meetings to AE opportunities at high rates
- Has run their own outbound experiments (new sequences, new personas, new channels)
- Understanding of the ICP beyond what they were told — has formed their own view on best-fit accounts
- Experience booking enterprise-level executives (VP+, C-suite)

### Seniority Signals

- **Entry → Senior SDR:** Consistently hits quota 3+ months running; owns their sequence library; coaches newer SDRs informally
- **Senior → SDR Manager:** Has run team processes (sequence reviews, call scoring) without being asked; wants to develop people, not just hit numbers
- **SDR → AE path:** The natural progression. Green flags: top quota attainment (top 20-25%), curiosity about the full sales cycle, has sat in on AE deals

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Cold call roleplay | Live: call the interviewer cold, pitch the company | Opener, handling objections, asking for the meeting |
| Email review | Write a cold outreach email to [target persona] in 10 minutes | Personalisation, value proposition, CTA |
| Activity & attainment | Walk me through your quota and attainment for each quarter | Accountability, self-awareness, trend |
| Resilience scenario | Tell me about your worst week in sales — what did you do? | Mindset, recovery, self-management |
| Process discussion | Walk me through your typical daily prospecting process | Structure, discipline, prioritisation |

**Best live test:** Give them your actual ICP, a real (or real-looking) LinkedIn profile, and ask them to cold call you as that prospect. You learn more in 3 minutes than a 30-minute interview.

### Red Flags
- Activity is low and they explain it away — SDRs have one metric: pipeline generated
- Quota attainment is consistently below 70% with no clear explanation or learning
- Cannot handle a basic objection ("we already have a tool") in the roleplay
- Has never built or refined their own outreach sequence
- "I prefer email to calling" — channel aversion at SDR level is a limiter
- Job-hopped multiple SDR roles with no progression toward AE
