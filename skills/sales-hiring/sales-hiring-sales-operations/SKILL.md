# Sales Operations — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Quota / scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Associate / Representative** | 0–2 years | Defined activity targets, pipeline contribution | Activity-based (calls, meetings set) |
| **Account Executive / Manager** | 2–5 years | Full sales cycle or named account set | Quota-carrying, individual contributor |
| **Senior / Lead** | 5–8 years | Complex deals, strategic accounts, peer mentoring | Higher quota, larger accounts |
| **Manager** | 5–8 years | Team quota, rep coaching and development | Team target, hiring input |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Segment or regional revenue, team of managers | Segment quota, hiring/strategy |
| **VP / Head** | 12+ years | Full revenue function or major segment, exec reporting | Company/segment quota, org design |
| **CRO** | 15+ years | All revenue: sales + CS + partnerships | Board-level accountability |

**Key sales seniority signal:** Quota attainment history. Ask for specific numbers: attainment % at each role, deal sizes, ACV, sales cycle length. High performers will answer readily. Evasion is a red flag.

## Sales Methodology Reference

Common frameworks you will encounter in interviews — knowing these helps evaluate depth:

| Framework | What it is | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **MEDDIC / MEDDPICC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion (+ Paper Process, Competition) — enterprise qualification | Enterprise AEs, Sales Leaders |
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline — simpler qualification for SMB/mid-market | SDRs, Inside Sales, Mid-Market AEs |
| **Challenger Sale** | Teach, Tailor, Take Control — leads with insight rather than discovery | Mid-market to enterprise AEs |
| **SPIN Selling** | Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff — consultative discovery | AEs across segments |
| **SPICED** | Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision — used in SaaS | SDRs, AEs, CS |
| **Command of the Message** | Force Management framework — value-based selling, connecting to business outcomes | Sales orgs with formal methodologies |
| **MEDDICC** | MEDDIC + Competition field | Enterprise orgs |

## Sales Operations

### What This Role Does
Sales operations professionals build and maintain the systems, data, and processes that enable sales teams to operate at full effectiveness. They own CRM administration, pipeline reporting, territory and quota design, compensation plan modelling, and forecasting infrastructure. Sales ops is the difference between a sales team flying blind and one with clear visibility into where deals are, why they're winning or losing, and what to do about it.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Sales Operations Specialist, CRM Administrator, Sales Analyst |
| Manager | Sales Operations Manager, Sales Ops Manager |
| Senior | Senior Sales Operations Manager, Head of Sales Operations |
| Director | Director of Sales Operations, VP Sales Operations |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Salesforce administration: objects, fields, workflows, validation rules, reports, dashboards — hands-on, not just user-level
- Pipeline reporting: can build accurate pipeline and forecast reports that sales leadership trusts
- Data hygiene: deduplication, lead-to-opportunity conversion, contact/account data quality
- Process design: documents and enforces sales processes — lead routing, opportunity stages, exit criteria
- Quota and territory management: understands quota-setting logic and territory carving principles

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Compensation modelling: builds and models comp plan scenarios — quota-to-OTE ratios, accelerators, SPIFFs
- Forecasting models: builds bottom-up and top-down forecast models; runs forecast calls with sales leadership
- Sales tech stack management: Outreach/Salesloft, ZoomInfo, Gong, Clari, or similar — administration and adoption
- SQL or Excel/Sheets advanced: custom analysis beyond out-of-the-box CRM reporting
- Win/loss analysis: structured programmes to understand why deals are won or lost

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Salesforce development (Apex, SOQL) or declarative automation (Flow, Process Builder)
- Revenue intelligence tools: Clari, Boostup, Aviso — forecasting automation and AI
- CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote): Salesforce CPQ, DealHub — for complex pricing models
- Data warehouse integration: Salesforce to Snowflake/BigQuery for advanced analytics

### Seniority Signals

- **Specialist → Manager:** Moves from maintaining existing reports to building new analytical frameworks; owns the forecast process, not just the data
- **Manager → Director:** Designs the sales tech stack strategy; contributes to comp plan design; partners with CFO on revenue modelling

### Red Flags
- "I manage the CRM" but has never built a custom Salesforce report from scratch
- No compensation modelling experience — crucial for senior Sales Ops
- Data hygiene is an afterthought — "we clean it when we need to"
- Cannot explain what a good forecast process looks like
- Has never run a win/loss analysis programme
