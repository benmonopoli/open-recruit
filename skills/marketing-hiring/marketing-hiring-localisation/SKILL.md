## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Localisation

### What This Role Does
Localisation (L10n) specialists adapt content, products, and marketing for international markets — going beyond translation to ensure cultural relevance, regulatory compliance, and local market resonance. At scale, this involves managing translation management systems (TMS), vendor networks, and internal stakeholders across multiple time zones. For global companies, localisation is a growth function, not just a translation service.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Localisation Specialist, Translation Coordinator, L10n Specialist |
| Manager | Localisation Manager, Internationalisation Manager, L10n Programme Manager |
| Senior | Senior Localisation Manager, Global Content Manager |
| Director | Head of Localisation, Director of Internationalisation, Director of Global Marketing |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Native or near-native fluency in at least one target language plus English
- Cultural adaptation knowledge: understands what localisation means beyond word-for-word translation
- TMS tools: Phrase, Lokalise, Crowdin, Smartcat, or Transifex — hands-on experience
- Quality assurance: can review translated content for accuracy, tone, and cultural fit
- Vendor management: briefing, reviewing, and managing external translators or agencies

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Multi-language programme management: coordinating 5+ language tracks simultaneously
- Internationalisation (i18n) awareness: can work with engineering on string extraction, plural forms, date/currency formatting
- CAT tools: SDL Trados, memoQ, or similar — for linguistically technical roles
- Local market knowledge: understands search behaviour, platform preferences, and content formats by region
- Localisation metrics: translation velocity, quality scores, cost per word

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Multiple language pairs (3+ languages)
- Machine translation post-editing (MTPE) programme management
- SEO localisation: transcreating keywords for local search intent
- Legal/regulatory localisation: privacy, terms, GDPR-compliant language by region

### Seniority Signals

- **Specialist → Manager:** Moves from managing translations to managing the localisation programme — vendor selection, quality frameworks, workflow automation
- **Manager → Director:** Owns the global content strategy; builds i18n processes with engineering; drives localisation as a competitive advantage

### Red Flags
- Treats localisation as translation only — no cultural or contextual adaptation
- Has only managed one language pair
- No TMS experience — spreadsheet-based localisation at scale is a bottleneck
- No understanding of i18n: has never worked with engineering on string handling
- Quality review process is informal or absent
