## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Enterprise Marketing / ABM

### What This Role Does
Enterprise and Account-Based Marketing (ABM) practitioners drive demand and pipeline from named target accounts — typically large companies with long sales cycles. ABM flips traditional marketing (generate many leads, filter down) to a targeted model: identify the right accounts, then run personalised, multi-touch campaigns to engage buying committees. Requires tight sales-marketing alignment and a data-driven approach.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | ABM Manager, Enterprise Marketing Manager, Demand Generation Manager |
| Senior | Senior ABM Manager, Senior Enterprise Marketing Manager |
| Director | Director of ABM, Head of Enterprise Marketing, Demand Generation Director |
| Specialist | Field Marketing Manager (ABM-focused), Account Marketer |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- ABM platforms: 6sense, Demandbase, Terminus, or RollWorks — hands-on experience, not just awareness
- ICP definition and target account list (TAL) development
- Sales-marketing alignment: has worked closely with AEs and SDRs, not just handed leads over
- Multi-channel campaign execution: email, LinkedIn, display, direct mail, events in a coordinated ABM motion
- Pipeline attribution: can connect ABM activity to opportunities and revenue

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- One-to-one, one-to-few, one-to-many ABM programme design
- Buying committee mapping: identifies and addresses multiple stakeholders in a target account
- Intent data: using G2, Bombora, or built-in intent signals to prioritise accounts
- ABM reporting: account engagement scoring, pipeline influence, velocity metrics
- Personalisation at scale: dynamic content, personalised landing pages (Mutiny, Intellimize)

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Enterprise deal involvement: has sat in on account strategy meetings alongside sales
- ABM programme built from scratch (greenfield experience)
- CRM administration: Salesforce campaign tracking, custom ABM reporting dashboards

### Seniority Signals

- **Manager → Senior:** Moves from running ABM campaigns to designing the programme architecture and reporting framework
- **Senior → Director:** Owns the full ABM tech stack; drives sales alignment at the leadership level; connects ABM directly to revenue outcomes

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Programme design | Design an ABM programme for [our ICP]. Walk me through the approach | Strategic thinking, platform knowledge |
| Tech stack discussion | Walk me through an ABM tech stack you've managed | Hands-on depth, not just awareness |
| Sales alignment | How do you ensure sales uses what marketing produces? | Collaboration, influence skills |
| Results review | What's the best pipeline outcome you've driven through ABM? | Commercial accountability |

### Red Flags
- "ABM is just targeted email" — no understanding of multi-channel, multi-touch orchestration
- No direct experience with ABM platforms
- Cannot articulate the difference between one-to-one and one-to-many ABM
- Weak sales relationship — has never sat in on a sales call or QBR
- Measures success in MQLs only — ABM success is account engagement and pipeline, not lead volume
