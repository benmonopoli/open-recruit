# Influencer Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Influencer Marketing

### What This Role Does
Influencer marketers build and manage creator and influencer partnerships to reach target audiences authentically. The role spans creator identification, brief writing, campaign execution, contract negotiation, and performance measurement. At B2C companies this spans macro influencers to micro/nano creators. At B2B companies, it increasingly involves industry thought leaders, LinkedIn creators, and niche community figures.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Influencer Coordinator, Creator Partnerships Coordinator |
| Manager | Influencer Marketing Manager, Creator Marketing Manager, Influencer Partnerships Manager |
| Senior | Senior Influencer Manager, Head of Influencer Marketing |
| Director | Director of Creator Partnerships, Head of Influencer & Partnerships |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Creator identification: beyond follower count — engagement rate, audience quality, content fit
- Brief writing: clear creative briefs that give creators enough direction without killing authenticity
- Influencer platforms: AspireIQ, Grin, Upfluence, Creator.co, or manual outreach at smaller scale
- Contract and rate negotiation: industry rate awareness, exclusivity, usage rights, deliverables
- FTC/ASA compliance: knows the rules around disclosure and branded content

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Micro/nano influencer programme management: scaling with smaller creators vs. big-name deals
- Performance measurement: CPM, CPE, earned media value (EMV), attributable clicks/sales
- Affiliate + influencer integration: connecting influencer activity to trackable revenue
- B2B influencer (LinkedIn): identifying and activating thought leaders for B2B audiences
- Long-term ambassador programme design

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Creator community building (Discord, Slack for brand advocates)
- UGC (user-generated content) strategy — scaling creator content beyond paid partnerships
- International influencer programmes across multiple markets
- Creator talent relationships at scale (100+ active creator roster)

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Runs campaigns end-to-end from identification to reporting; manages creator relationships independently
- **Manager → Director:** Builds and scales the influencer programme; connects influencer to revenue; manages cross-channel integration with paid and organic

### Red Flags
- Focuses only on follower count — no audience quality analysis
- No understanding of engagement rate vs. reach tradeoffs
- Cannot speak to FTC/ASA disclosure requirements
- Has only worked with one or two large influencer deals — no programme management experience
- No measurable business outcome from influencer spend
