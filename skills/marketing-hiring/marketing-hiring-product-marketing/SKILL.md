# Product Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Product Marketing

### What This Role Does
Product marketers (PMMs) are the connective tissue between product, sales, and the market. They own positioning, messaging, competitive intelligence, product launches, and sales enablement. The best PMMs deeply understand both the product and the customer — and can translate between them. Often described as "the voice of the customer inside the company and the voice of the product to the market."

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Product Marketing Coordinator, Associate PMM |
| Manager | Product Marketing Manager (PMM), Product Marketer |
| Senior | Senior PMM, Senior Product Marketing Manager |
| Director | Director of Product Marketing, Head of Product Marketing |
| VP+ | VP Product Marketing, Chief Marketing Officer (with PMM background) |
| Specialist | Competitive Intelligence Manager, Market Intelligence Analyst |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Messaging and positioning: can write clear, differentiated, customer-centric messaging
- Customer and market research: interviews, surveys, win/loss analysis
- Product launch execution: coordinating cross-functional teams to ship and announce
- Sales enablement: battle cards, pitch decks, objection handling guides
- Cross-functional collaboration — PMM is inherently a role that requires influence without authority

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Competitive intelligence: systematic competitor tracking, feature comparisons, positioning maps
- ICP definition and persona development grounded in real data
- Pricing and packaging input (senior+)
- Analyst relations: Gartner, Forrester, G2 — especially for B2B/enterprise
- Go-to-market strategy: market segmentation, channel selection, launch sequencing

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Category creation experience
- Experience taking a product from early adopters to mainstream
- Self-serve product marketing (PLG context) vs. sales-assisted (enterprise context)
- Technical depth: comfortable reading product roadmaps and talking credibly to engineers

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Can run a product launch end-to-end; produces usable sales materials without heavy editing
- **Manager → Senior:** Owns the messaging framework for an entire product line; drives win/loss programme with real findings that change decisions
- **Senior → Director:** Sets PMM strategy across the portfolio; manages a team of PMMs; influences product roadmap based on market intelligence

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Messaging exercise | Take-home: write a one-pager positioning [product] for [audience], 2 hours | Clarity, customer-centricity, differentiation |
| Launch debrief | Walk me through a launch you owned start to finish | Cross-functional process, how they handled ambiguity |
| Competitive deep dive | How would you approach competitive intel for [our market]? | Research instincts, analytical rigour |
| Sales enablement review | Review a battle card or deck they've built | Practical output quality |
| Stakeholder scenario | Product team wants to launch in 2 weeks, sales wants 3 months of runway — how do you navigate? | Influence skills, judgement |

### Red Flags
- Can't articulate the difference between features and benefits
- "I write the website copy" — execution only, no strategic thinking
- No customer research experience — positioning without customer data is just guessing
- Has never sat in on a sales call or customer interview
- Messaging is jargon-heavy and internally focused rather than customer-centric
