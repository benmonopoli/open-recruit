# Content Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Content Marketing

### What This Role Does
Content marketers create and distribute valuable content that attracts, educates, and retains audiences. The best content marketers think like publishers — editorial strategy, audience research, distribution, and performance measurement — not just writers. At SEO-led companies (like Ahrefs), content and SEO are deeply intertwined.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Content Writer, Content Coordinator, Blog Writer, Junior Content Marketer |
| Manager | Content Marketing Manager, Content Strategist, Content Manager |
| Senior | Senior Content Marketing Manager, Senior Content Strategist, Editorial Manager |
| Director | Director of Content, Head of Content, Content Marketing Director |
| VP+ | VP Content, Chief Content Officer |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Demonstrably strong writing — long-form, clear, audience-aware
- SEO fundamentals: keyword research, search intent, on-page basics
- Editorial discipline: deadlines, editing others' work, maintaining brand voice
- Content analytics: GA4, understanding traffic, engagement, conversion metrics
- CMS proficiency: WordPress, Webflow, Contentful, or similar

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Content strategy: topic clustering, content calendar ownership, funnel-stage mapping
- Distribution and amplification: email, social, syndication, link building
- Interviewing subject matter experts and turning conversations into polished content
- Understanding content ROI: attributing pipeline or revenue to content
- Multimedia content: video scripts, podcast content, visual content briefs

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Audience building: newsletter growth, community development
- Data-driven content: original research, surveys, data storytelling
- Managing freelancers or agencies at scale
- Ahrefs/SEMrush power user (tool fluency for SEO-content teams)

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Can own a content calendar end-to-end without prompting; understands why they're writing something, not just what
- **Manager → Senior:** Sets the content strategy, not just executes it; can demonstrate traffic or conversion impact from their work
- **Senior → Director:** Builds and manages a content team; connects content to revenue pipeline; has a defensible POV on owned media strategy

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio review | Review 3–5 pieces of their published work before the call | Quality, range, SEO awareness, storytelling |
| Writing task | Take-home: draft a 500-word article intro on a given topic, 60–90 min | Voice, structure, research instinct |
| Strategy discussion | How would you build a content programme for [company] in 90 days? | Strategic thinking vs. execution focus |
| Analytics walkthrough | Talk me through the performance of a piece of content you're proud of | Data literacy, attribution thinking |

### Red Flags
- Writing portfolio is thin, generic, or all the same format
- No understanding of search intent — writes "for people" without thinking about how people find it
- Cannot speak to traffic, engagement, or business impact of their content
- "I just write, SEO is separate" — acceptable at junior level only
- Edits slowly or doesn't self-edit — content is a volume game at most companies
