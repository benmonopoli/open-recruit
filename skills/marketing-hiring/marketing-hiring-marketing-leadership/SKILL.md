# Marketing Leadership — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Marketing Leadership

### What This Role Does
Marketing leaders (VP Marketing, CMO, Head of Marketing) own the full marketing function — strategy, team, budget, and business outcomes. They operate at the intersection of brand, demand, and product. The best marketing leaders are both strategic (where to play, how to win) and commercially sharp (pipeline, CAC, LTV, payback period). At growth-stage companies, they often need to be hands-on while building a team.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Director | Director of Marketing, Marketing Director |
| VP | VP Marketing, VP of Growth, VP of Brand & Marketing |
| Head | Head of Marketing |
| C-Suite | Chief Marketing Officer (CMO), Chief Growth Officer (CGO) |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Full-funnel thinking: brand awareness through to revenue and retention
- Budget management and allocation across channels with ROI discipline
- Team building: hired, developed, and managed a marketing team
- Executive communication: board decks, investor narrative, CEO alignment
- Commercial literacy: understands CAC, LTV, payback period, MQL/SQL, pipeline contribution

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Proven demand generation engine: has built and scaled a repeatable pipeline programme
- Brand-building alongside performance: understands long and short marketing
- PLG (Product-Led Growth) experience — increasingly expected at SaaS companies
- Analyst/PR relationships at scale
- International marketing experience

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Category creation: has defined or led entry into a new market category
- IPO or acquisition marketing experience
- Strong external brand: public speaking, published POV, industry recognition
- Has managed agency relationships and in-house teams simultaneously

### Seniority Signals

- **Director → VP:** Moves from owning a function to owning the full marketing P&L; makes hiring decisions, not just executes them; reports to CEO/COO directly
- **VP → CMO:** Takes external-facing responsibility for brand reputation; sits on leadership team; has built a team of directors and VPs, not just managers
- **Key signal:** Ask "How have you allocated a marketing budget and what drove your choices?" Great candidates give a specific, data-grounded answer. Weak candidates give a generic framework without numbers.

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic vision | 30/60/90 day plan presentation | Prioritisation, speed to insight, stakeholder management |
| Budget deep dive | Walk me through a marketing budget you owned | Commercial fluency, ROI thinking |
| Team building | How have you structured and scaled a marketing team? | Talent philosophy, hiring judgement |
| Growth narrative | Walk me through a company where you built a demand engine | Specific results, attribution thinking |
| CEO/Board alignment | How do you manage up and communicate marketing's value? | Executive presence, commercial translation |

### Red Flags
- Cannot speak fluently to pipeline metrics and CAC
- Has only managed large, established marketing teams — no scaling experience
- Brand-only or performance-only background with no bridge between them
- Weak hiring track record — previous teams were not high-performing
- Over-indexes on press coverage and awards as success metrics
