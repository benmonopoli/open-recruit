# PR & Communications — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## PR & Communications

### What This Role Does
PR and communications professionals manage the company's narrative with external audiences: press, analysts, investors, customers, and the public. The discipline spans media relations (journalists, publications), thought leadership (executive positioning, speaking), crisis communications, and internal communications. The best PR professionals are storytellers who understand what makes news and can translate business achievements into compelling narratives.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | PR Coordinator, Communications Coordinator, Media Relations Coordinator |
| Manager | PR Manager, Communications Manager, Media Relations Manager |
| Senior | Senior PR Manager, Head of PR, Director of Communications |
| Director | Director of PR, VP Communications, Chief Communications Officer |
| Specialist | Corporate Communications Manager, Investor Relations Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Media relationships: active journalist relationships in relevant beats (tech, SaaS, industry verticals)
- Press release writing: AP style, newsworthiness judgement, quote crafting
- Pitch writing: concise, compelling, targeted media pitches — knows what makes a journalist respond
- Crisis communications: can draft a holding statement and manage an unfolding story
- Media monitoring: Meltwater, Cision, Mention, or manual monitoring for coverage

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Analyst relations: Gartner, Forrester, IDC — briefing analysts, managing Magic Quadrant/Wave processes
- Executive thought leadership: ghostwriting, speaking programme management, award submissions
- Earned media strategy: connecting PR activity to business goals (not just clip counts)
- Social media amplification of earned coverage
- International PR: managing PR agencies in multiple markets

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Investor relations experience (for public or pre-IPO companies)
- Crisis communications track record — specifically having navigated a real, public-facing crisis
- Podcast PR: securing executive placements on relevant podcasts
- Community journalism background (credibility with journalists)

### Red Flags
- Journalist relationships are transactional and dormant — can't name a journalist they've placed a story with recently
- Measures PR success in press release distribution — no earned coverage focus
- Has never managed a crisis — ask specifically; the quality of their answer reveals a lot
- No understanding of newsworthiness — pitches everything, lands nothing
- PR is "getting us in the press" without connection to business goals
