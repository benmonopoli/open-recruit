- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Performance Marketing / Paid Media

### What This Role Does
Performance marketers manage paid advertising channels with a focus on measurable outcomes: clicks, leads, pipeline, and revenue. The role spans search (Google/Bing Ads), paid social (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok), programmatic display, and increasingly connected TV. Strong performance marketers combine analytical rigour (attribution, bidding strategy, budget allocation) with creative instincts (ad copy, visual testing). Attribution is increasingly complex post-iOS changes — cookie deprecation awareness is now essential.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | PPC Specialist, Paid Media Specialist, Paid Search Analyst |
| Manager | Performance Marketing Manager, Paid Media Manager, PPC Manager |
| Senior | Senior Performance Marketing Manager, Head of Paid Media |
| Director | Director of Performance Marketing, VP Growth |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Google Ads: Search, Display, Performance Max — campaign setup, bidding strategy, Quality Score optimisation
- Meta Ads: campaign structure, audience targeting, creative testing, iOS 14+ measurement impact
- Analytics: GA4, UTM tracking, attribution modelling — can build their own dashboards and interpret channel data
- Budget management: ROAS targets, CPA targets, budget allocation across channels
- A/B testing: creative, landing page, audience — structured test and learn methodology

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- LinkedIn Ads: B2B targeting, lead gen forms, Sponsored Content — especially relevant for SaaS
- Programmatic: The Trade Desk, DV360, or DSP experience
- Attribution modelling: MTA (multi-touch), MMM (media mix modelling) — post-cookie awareness
- Landing page CRO: can identify and fix landing page conversion issues
- Feed management: Google Merchant Center, dynamic ads for e-commerce

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- TikTok Ads, Pinterest Ads, connected TV (CTV)
- Paid social creative strategy: briefs for video ads, carousel ads — not just copy
- Marketing mix modelling (MMM) experience
- Python or SQL for paid media data analysis at scale

### Red Flags
- Only manages one platform — performance marketers need channel breadth
- No understanding of attribution post-iOS 14 — "we use last-click"
- Cannot speak to CPA, ROAS, and how they make budget allocation decisions
- Spend scale is very small — managing £5k/month budgets is very different from £500k/month
- No landing page or CRO awareness — paid media without conversion focus wastes spend
