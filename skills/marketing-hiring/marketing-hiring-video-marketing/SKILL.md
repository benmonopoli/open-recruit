# Video Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Video Marketing

### What This Role Does
Video marketers create and distribute video content to drive awareness, engagement, and conversion. The discipline spans strategy (channel and format decisions), scripting, production (filming, editing), distribution (YouTube, social, paid), and performance measurement. The best video marketers think in formats — they know when to use long-form YouTube, short-form Reels/TikTok, product demos, or documentary-style storytelling, and why.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Video Producer, Video Content Creator, Video Coordinator |
| Manager | Video Marketing Manager, YouTube Manager, Video Content Manager |
| Senior | Senior Video Marketing Manager, Head of Video |
| Director | Director of Video, Head of Video Content |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Video production: filming, lighting, audio — can produce professional-quality video independently or with minimal support
- Video editing: Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or Final Cut — colour grading, audio mixing, motion graphics basics
- Scripting: can write clear, engaging video scripts for different formats and audiences
- YouTube channel management: upload optimisation, thumbnails, titles, descriptions, playlists, analytics
- Short-form video: Reels, TikTok, Shorts — understands the format differences

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Video SEO: YouTube keyword research, title/description optimisation, CTR strategy
- Channel strategy: content pillars, upload cadence, audience growth strategy
- Thumbnail design: high-CTR thumbnail creation — Canva or Photoshop
- Video analytics: YouTube Studio, watch time, audience retention, click-through rate — data-driven iteration
- Motion graphics: After Effects, Canva — animated titles, lower thirds, product overlays

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- On-camera comfort: can present to camera, not just produce
- Live streaming: YouTube Live, LinkedIn Live — event and product launch streaming
- Podcast video: recording, editing, and distributing video podcasts
- Technical tutorial video production (especially relevant for developer-audience companies)
- Own YouTube channel with demonstrated growth

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Can own the full video workflow from brief to published — not just production, but strategy and distribution
- **Manager → Director:** Demonstrates measurable channel growth and video contribution to business goals; manages a team or freelancer network; owns video as a channel, not just a format

### Red Flags
- Portfolio is only polished corporate videos — no platform-native content
- No understanding of YouTube analytics — produces content without watching performance data
- Production quality obsession without distribution strategy — "I make beautiful videos that nobody watches"
- Has never managed a YouTube channel end-to-end
- Cannot explain video SEO or why thumbnail CTR matters
