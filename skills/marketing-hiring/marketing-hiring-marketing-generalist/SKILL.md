# Marketing Generalist — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Marketing Generalist

### What This Role Does
Marketing generalists span multiple channels and functions — most common at early-stage companies where one person needs to cover content, social, email, events, and paid in some combination. The value is breadth and adaptability. The risk is a mile wide and an inch deep — assess depth in at least 2–3 channels before hiring.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Marketing Coordinator, Marketing Assistant |
| Manager | Marketing Manager, Digital Marketing Manager |
| Senior | Senior Marketing Manager, Head of Marketing (small team) |
| Director | Marketing Director, Head of Marketing |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Demonstrable competency in at least 2–3 channels (content, email, social, paid, SEO)
- Project management: can run multiple campaigns simultaneously without dropping balls
- Analytics baseline: GA4, channel-specific dashboards, reporting to stakeholders
- Strong writing: copy across formats (email, social, ads, website)
- CRM basics: HubSpot, Mailchimp, or equivalent

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Campaign management end-to-end: ideation through measurement
- Budget management experience, even at small scale
- Cross-functional work: has coordinated with product, sales, or design
- Basic paid media: Google Ads or Meta Ads — enough to run campaigns independently
- ABM or demand generation basics (B2B context)

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Has built a marketing function from near-scratch
- Marketing automation: HubSpot workflows, Zapier integrations
- Technical enough to update website copy without engineering support

### Seniority Signals

- **Coordinator → Manager:** Can plan and execute a campaign from brief to report without step-by-step guidance
- **Manager → Senior/Head:** Demonstrates impact in at least one channel with data; has hired or managed freelancers/agencies; makes budget allocation decisions

### Interview & Assessment

| Stage | Format | What you're assessing |
|---|---|---|
| Channel depth | "Pick the channel you're strongest in — walk me through a campaign you ran" | Depth in at least one area |
| Breadth scan | "For each channel — content, email, SEO, paid, social — rate yourself 1–5 and explain" | Honest self-assessment, breadth |
| Prioritisation | "You have £10k and 30 days. We need leads. What do you do?" | Judgement, not just activity |
| Past metrics | Walk me through the best marketing result you've driven with data | Accountability and data literacy |

### Red Flags
- Breadth is claimed but no depth anywhere — "I've done a bit of everything" with no stand-out
- No metrics or results from any role
- Has only worked at large companies where the "generalist" role was actually very narrow
- Cannot make a prioritisation call — wants to do everything at once
