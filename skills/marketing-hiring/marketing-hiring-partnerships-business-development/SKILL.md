- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Partnerships & Business Development

### What This Role Does
Partnership marketers build and activate commercial and co-marketing relationships with complementary companies, platforms, agencies, and resellers. The discipline spans technology integrations, channel programmes, affiliate programmes, and co-marketing (joint content, webinars, events). Different from pure BD (which closes commercial deals) — partnership marketers focus on activating and growing the partner ecosystem for mutual marketing benefit.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | Partnerships Manager, Partner Marketing Manager, Business Development Manager |
| Senior | Senior Partnerships Manager, Strategic Partnerships Manager |
| Director | Director of Partnerships, Head of Partnerships, VP Partnerships |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Partner identification: can systematically identify and qualify potential partners by strategic fit
- Relationship building: outbound partner outreach, building trust over time, multi-stakeholder management
- Co-marketing execution: joint webinars, content swaps, newsletter sponsorships, integration spotlights
- Commercial awareness: understands revenue sharing, referral models, and affiliate structures
- Partner programme management: tracking partner health, communication cadences, activation

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Technology partnership experience: API/integration partner programmes, marketplace listings (Salesforce AppExchange, HubSpot App Marketplace, Shopify App Store)
- Channel/reseller programme design: MDF allocation, deal registration, enablement materials
- Affiliate programme management: Impact.com, PartnerStack, ShareASale — tracking, payouts, fraud management
- Co-sell motion: has worked alongside sales to close partner-sourced deals
- Partner enablement: building partner portals, training programmes, co-branded assets

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Built a partner programme from scratch
- International partnership experience across multiple regions
- Strategic alliance experience at enterprise scale (GSI partnerships, OEM deals)
- Network of active partner contacts in a relevant industry

### Red Flags
- Relies on warm introductions only — no outbound partnership development capability
- Can't demonstrate pipeline or revenue attributed to partnerships
- No follow-through: lots of signed partnerships, few activated ones
- Does not understand the partner's business model or what's in it for them
