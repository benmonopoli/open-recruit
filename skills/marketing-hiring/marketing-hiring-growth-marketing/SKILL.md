# Growth Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Growth Marketing

### What This Role Does
Growth marketers focus on driving and optimising the full acquisition-activation-retention funnel through rapid experimentation. The discipline draws from marketing, product, and data — growth marketers are comfortable running A/B tests, reading funnel analytics, writing copy, and working with engineers on product experiments. The best growth marketers are T-shaped: broad channel awareness with depth in experimentation and data.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Growth Marketer, Growth Marketing Specialist, Growth Analyst |
| Manager | Growth Marketing Manager, Head of Growth Marketing |
| Senior | Senior Growth Manager, Growth Lead |
| Director | Head of Growth, Director of Growth, VP Growth |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Experimentation: structured A/B and multivariate testing — hypothesis, test design, statistical significance, learning extraction
- Funnel analytics: can map and analyse acquisition → activation → retention → revenue funnel
- Data fluency: Mixpanel, Amplitude, GA4, or similar — can pull their own analysis without a data analyst
- Cross-channel awareness: understands paid, organic, email, in-product touchpoints holistically
- SQL basics: enough to query their own data for funnel analysis

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Product-led growth: in-product virality, referral loops, freemium conversion optimisation
- CRO: landing page testing, onboarding flow optimisation, friction reduction
- Paid acquisition: enough to run growth experiments across paid channels
- Cohort analysis: retention curves, LTV by acquisition source, payback periods
- Growth modelling: can build simple models to forecast experiment outcomes

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Technical growth: has worked with engineers on product-level growth experiments
- Viral loop design: referral programmes, sharing mechanics, word-of-mouth amplification
- International growth: growth motions that work across markets
- Growth team building: has hired and scaled a growth team

### Red Flags
- "Growth hacking" in the title but no statistical rigour — running tests without significance is noise
- Cannot speak to a specific test they ran, the hypothesis, and what they concluded
- No data analysis skills — growth without data literacy is guesswork
- Has only worked in paid acquisition — real growth spans channels
- Chases short-term spikes rather than sustainable funnel improvements
