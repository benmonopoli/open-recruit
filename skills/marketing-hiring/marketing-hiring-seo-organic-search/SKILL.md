- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## SEO / Organic Search

### What This Role Does
SEO specialists grow organic search visibility and traffic through technical optimisation, content strategy, and link acquisition. The discipline spans technical SEO (site architecture, crawlability, Core Web Vitals), on-page (keyword targeting, content optimisation), and off-page (link building, digital PR). At SEO-led companies — particularly SEO tooling companies like Ahrefs — this is a core, highly scrutinised hire. Expect candidates to have strong opinions and demonstrable results.

| Technical track | Technical SEO Engineer, SEO Developer |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Keyword research: intent classification, search volume, difficulty assessment — tool fluency (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner)
- Technical SEO: crawlability, indexability, Core Web Vitals, structured data, canonical tags, sitemaps
- On-page optimisation: title tags, meta descriptions, header hierarchy, internal linking strategy
- Google Search Console and GA4: daily tools, can pull and interpret organic performance data
- Content strategy: can map keyword opportunities to content types and formats

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Link building: outreach-based, digital PR, broken link building, unlinked mentions — knows what actually works in 2025
- Log file analysis: SEO log analysis with Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, or custom scripts
- Python for SEO: data manipulation, bulk analysis, SERP scraping — increasingly expected at senior level
- International SEO: hreflang, ccTLDs, subdirectory vs. subdomain decisions
- E-E-A-T strategy: building topical authority, author credentials, trust signals

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Algorithm update forensics: can diagnose traffic changes after core updates
- Enterprise SEO: managing SEO at 1M+ URL scale
- Programmatic SEO: templated page creation for long-tail keyword capture
- JavaScript SEO: understanding how JS impacts rendering and indexation
- Published SEO research or case studies

### Seniority Signals

- **Specialist → Manager:** Can own a site's entire SEO roadmap independently; interprets algorithm updates without guidance
- **Manager → Senior:** Builds the SEO strategy that shapes content, technical, and link roadmaps simultaneously; has driven measurable traffic growth at scale
- **Senior → Head/Director:** Sets SEO as an organisational capability, not just a channel; connects organic to revenue and builds internal education for content and product teams

### Red Flags
- "I write content that ranks" — content without technical, link, or strategic understanding
- Relies on one tool (usually SEMrush) without understanding what the data means
- Cannot explain how Googlebot crawls and renders JavaScript
- Has never run a link building campaign — "I just create great content"
- No experience recovering from a Google algorithm update
- For Ahrefs specifically: a candidate who doesn't use Ahrefs is a serious red flag
