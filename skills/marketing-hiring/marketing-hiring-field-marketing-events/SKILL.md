# Field Marketing & Events — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

---

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Field Marketing & Events

### What This Role Does
Field marketers drive pipeline and brand awareness through in-person and virtual experiences: conferences, trade shows, executive dinners, webinars, roundtables, and owned events. The role is logistics-heavy but the best field marketers are pipeline-focused — every event should have a defined business outcome, not just a booth and branded swag.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Coordinator | Events Coordinator, Field Marketing Coordinator, Marketing Events Coordinator |
| Manager | Field Marketing Manager, Events Manager, Marketing Events Manager |
| Senior | Senior Field Marketing Manager, Head of Events |
| Director | Director of Field Marketing, Head of Field Marketing |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Event logistics: venue sourcing, vendor management, budgeting, on-site execution
- Sales alignment: events are only valuable if sales follows up — pre/post event coordination is critical
- Pipeline tracking: can attribute pipeline generated or influenced by events
- Webinar execution: Zoom Webinars, ON24, Goldcast, or similar — end-to-end management
- Budget management: negotiating with vendors, tracking spend, managing to budget

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Owned event design: has built a proprietary event (user conference, executive summit, roadshow)
- Lead capture and qualification: badge scanning, lead forms, post-event scoring
- Sponsorship negotiation: securing speaking slots and strategic positioning at third-party events
- Virtual event production: production values, interactive elements, audience engagement
- International event experience

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Community event programming (meetups, user groups)
- Event tech stack: Cvent, Splash, Bizzabo, Eventbrite — platform depth
- Executive engagement programmes: C-suite roundtables, advisory boards
- ABM event integration: personalised experiences for target accounts

### Red Flags
- "Events are about brand awareness" with no pipeline accountability
- Has never managed a budget independently
- Logistics-only focus — no demand generation or sales alignment experience
- No post-event follow-up process ("we hand leads to sales")
- Has only managed virtual events — no on-site experience (or vice versa)
