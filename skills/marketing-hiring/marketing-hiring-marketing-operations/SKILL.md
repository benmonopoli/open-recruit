- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Marketing Operations

### What This Role Does
Marketing operations professionals manage the technology, data, and processes that enable the marketing team to execute and measure effectively. They own the MarTech stack, attribution modelling, lead routing, campaign operations, and marketing data quality. Often the most technically-minded role in a marketing team — the bridge between marketing and IT/engineering. Increasingly overlaps with Revenue Operations (RevOps) at scale.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Specialist | Marketing Operations Specialist, MarTech Specialist, CRM Administrator |
| Manager | Marketing Operations Manager, RevOps Manager, MarTech Manager |
| Senior | Senior Marketing Operations Manager, Head of Marketing Ops |
| Director | Director of Marketing Operations, VP Revenue Operations |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- HubSpot or Marketo administration: campaigns, workflows, lead scoring, forms, CRM sync
- Salesforce CRM: SFDC basics, campaign influence reporting, lead/contact/opportunity objects
- Data hygiene: deduplication, normalisation, field mapping between systems
- Attribution reporting: first-touch, last-touch, multi-touch — can build custom attribution models
- Campaign operations: UTM tagging standards, campaign naming conventions, reporting cadence

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Lead routing: round-robin, territory-based, intent-based routing — LeanData, Chili Piper, or custom
- Marketing automation architecture: complex multi-step workflows, lead lifecycle management
- BI/reporting tools: Tableau, Looker, or similar — custom marketing dashboards beyond platform defaults
- CDP experience: Segment, Rudderstack — customer data unification
- SQL: custom reporting, data extraction from data warehouse

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Custom API integrations between MarTech tools
- Marketing data warehouse: Snowflake/BigQuery with marketing data models
- ABM platform administration: 6sense, Demandbase
- GDPR/CCPA compliance management in MarTech stack

### Red Flags
- Cannot explain lead scoring and why it matters
- HubSpot/Marketo experience is "I send emails" — no workflow or CRM integration depth
- No Salesforce exposure for B2B roles (nearly universal in SaaS)
- No attribution modelling experience — "we look at last click"
- Data hygiene is not a priority — "we clean up when there's time"
