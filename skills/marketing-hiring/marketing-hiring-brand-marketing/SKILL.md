# Brand Marketing — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Brand Marketing

### What This Role Does
Brand marketers define and protect how a company is perceived — its positioning, personality, visual identity, and emotional resonance. The discipline spans brand strategy (what we stand for and who we're for), brand expression (how it looks and sounds), and brand campaigns (building awareness and affinity over time). Often misunderstood as "soft" — strong brand is measurable through brand equity studies, share of voice, and long-term pricing power.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Manager | Brand Manager, Brand Marketing Manager |
| Senior | Senior Brand Manager, Senior Brand Marketer |
| Director | Brand Director, Head of Brand, Director of Brand Marketing |
| VP+ | VP Brand, Chief Brand Officer |
| Adjacent | Brand Strategist, Brand Communications Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Brand positioning: can develop and articulate a clear, differentiated brand strategy
- Brand guidelines: has developed or maintained brand identity guidelines across visual and verbal dimensions
- Integrated campaign management: coordinates brand campaigns across channels with agencies and in-house teams
- Market and consumer research: brand equity studies, NPS, customer perception research
- Creative briefing: can write a clear creative brief that produces the right output from agencies

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Brand measurement: share of voice, aided/unaided awareness, brand sentiment tracking
- Agency management: selecting, briefing, and managing brand and creative agencies
- Brand architecture: masterbrand vs. house of brands decisions, sub-brand management
- Campaign production: photography, video, out-of-home — production process management
- Employer branding: EVP development, candidate experience alignment with brand

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Category creation or brand repositioning at scale
- Sponsorship and partnership brand management
- Brand during M&A: integration or transition brand management
- Internal brand: culture-brand alignment, internal communications strategy

### Red Flags
- Confuses brand with design or social media management
- Cannot explain how they measure brand effectiveness
- Has never managed or briefed an agency
- Brand guidelines produced but never enforced or adopted
- All brand work is vanity metrics: awards, press coverage, follower counts
