# Developer Relations / DevRel — Hiring Guide

## How to Use This Guide

- **Sourcing:** Use the title synonyms and Boolean strings in each section
- **Screening:** Skill tiers: T1 = must-have, T2 = differentiator, T3 = rare upside
- **Interviewing:** Use the assessment approach to design your interview loop
- **Benchmarking:** Comp ranges are US market, growth-stage (Series B–D). Adjust for location, company stage, and current market

## Universal Seniority Framework

| Level | Typical YoE | What they own | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coordinator / Associate** | 0–2 years | Executes defined tasks, supports campaigns | Self |
| **Manager / Specialist** | 2–5 years | Owns a channel or programme end-to-end | Channel / function |
| **Senior Manager / Senior** | 5–8 years | Drives strategy for a channel, leads small teams or agencies | Team / function |
| **Director** | 8–12 years | Owns a marketing function (e.g. demand gen, brand), manages managers | Department |
| **VP / Head of Marketing** | 12+ years | Full marketing function ownership, budget, exec reporting | Company |
| **CMO** | 15+ years | Company-wide marketing strategy, board-level, often P&L exposure | Company + external |

**Marketing-specific promotion signal:** Moving from execution to strategy. The jump from Senior to Director is often the hardest — it requires comfort with ambiguity, managing through influence, and operating with incomplete data.

## Developer Relations / DevRel

### What This Role Does
Developer relations professionals bridge a company and its developer audience — through technical content (tutorials, documentation, blog posts), conference speaking, community engagement, and API/SDK adoption. For companies whose users or buyers are developers — including SEO tools with technical users, developer tools, APIs, and infrastructure products — DevRel is a growth function, not just a marketing one. The best DevRel engineers are practitioners first: they write real code and engage authentically.

### Titles & Synonyms

| Level | Common titles |
|---|---|
| Junior | Developer Advocate, Junior DevRel Engineer |
| Mid | Developer Advocate, Developer Relations Engineer |
| Senior | Senior Developer Advocate, Senior DevRel Engineer |
| Director | Head of Developer Relations, Developer Relations Director, VP DevRel |
| Adjacent | Developer Experience Manager (DX), Technical Community Manager |

### Skill Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-have:**
- Technical credibility: can write real code in at least one language; has built and shipped something
- Technical content: tutorials, code samples, blog posts, documentation — can produce content that a developer trusts
- Developer empathy: understands the developer experience from the inside — setup friction, API design, documentation gaps
- Public speaking: comfortable presenting at conferences and meetups — technical talks, not generic marketing presentations
- Developer community presence: GitHub activity, Stack Overflow, Discord, Hacker News — is a participant in developer culture

**Tier 2 — Differentiator:**
- Developer content at scale: YouTube channel, blog following, podcast, newsletter — an owned developer audience
- SDK/API advocacy: driving adoption through integrations, tutorials, and sample applications
- Community programme management: developer ambassador or champion programmes
- Product feedback loop: structured process for turning developer pain points into product improvements
- Open source experience: contributions to or maintenance of relevant open source projects

**Tier 3 — Bonus:**
- Framework or tool authorship
- Keynote speaking experience at major developer conferences (KubeCon, PyCon, JSConf, etc.)
- Strong personal brand in a developer community
- Multilingual technical content creation

### Seniority Signals

- **Junior → Mid:** Creates content independently; presents at meetups without scaffolding; generates real developer engagement
- **Mid → Senior:** Has an owned developer audience; drives measurable API/SDK adoption; influences product roadmap based on developer feedback
- **Senior → Director:** Builds the DevRel function and team; connects developer engagement to business metrics; represents the company in the developer ecosystem at a strategic level

### Red Flags
- Technical credibility is thin — "I talk about technical things" but can't write code
- Content is polished but shallow — not written from genuine practitioner experience
- Conference talks are about the company's product, not developer education
- No GitHub presence or developer community activity
- Cannot articulate how DevRel drives business outcomes (not just community goodwill)
