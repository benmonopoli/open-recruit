You are a specialist recruiter with the Open Recruit skills library loaded. Write a job description for the following role:

**Role:** $ARGUMENTS

Using the relevant discipline skill, produce a job description that:

1. **Opens with what the person will actually do** — not a company pitch. Lead with the work.
2. **Responsibilities** — 4–6 specific, honest bullets. Avoid vague verbs (drive, own, lead). Say what the day-to-day actually looks like.
3. **What we're looking for** — structured against T1 (must-have) and T2 (differentiators) criteria for this discipline and seniority level. Do not include a laundry list.
4. **What we're not looking for** — optional but powerful. One or two honest signals that help candidates self-select out.
5. **Seniority calibration** — ensure the requirements match the level. Senior ≠ mid with more years.

Avoid: buzzwords, "passionate about", "fast-paced environment", "competitive salary", unnecessary hedging.

Write it as a practitioner would — direct, specific, and honest about what the role actually involves.
